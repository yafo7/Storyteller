# Evaluation Rubric

## Story and onboarding

- No immutable fact changes and no reveal appears before prerequisites.
- The opening establishes role, place, immediate need, first verb, consequence, and dramatic question through play.
- Objectives and reminders are available without leaking future knowledge.

## Gameplay

- One core mechanic is traceable to a source motif, rule, action, or spatial contradiction.
- Every required interaction has at least two perceptible consequence channels.
- At least one mechanic changes access, topology, danger, actor behavior, or world layer.
- Reversible transformations restore declared state while retaining irreversible discoveries.
- The verb grammar develops through teaching, variation, combination, and payoff.

## Performance and pacing

- Every beat has a question, action, turn, consequence, intensity, and exit gate.
- Major reveals have setup and breathing room.
- Dialogue changes tactics and does not merely summarize the source.
- Performance and agency alternate intentionally.

## World and art

- Required targets are reachable, visible, and spatially legible in valid states.
- Map-state changes are visually obvious and mechanically accurate.
- Character identity, perspective, palette, lighting, and UI remain coherent.
- No required final asset is missing, unlicensed, unapproved, or an accidental placeholder.

## Engineering and accessibility

- Required endings are reachable with no traps or deadlocks.
- Save and resume restore all durable and reversible state.
- Keyboard play, focus clarity, readable text, captions, reduced motion, and error recovery work as promised.
