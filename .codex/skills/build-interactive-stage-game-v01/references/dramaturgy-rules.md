# Dramaturgy Rules

## Preserve the dramatic engine

Identify before writing scenes:

- The objective truth and irreversible events
- Each character's objective, obstacle, tactic, and current belief
- The audience question driving each scene
- The evidence that changes interpretation
- The central reveal and all prerequisite reveals
- The emotional turn ending each beat

Convert plot summaries into playable changes of state. Every beat should alter knowledge, position, possession, relationship, danger, access, or intent.

## Build reveal gates

Assign each fact an earliest reveal beat. Require evidence or prerequisite facts when appropriate. Enforce three separate layers:

1. Objective canon: what occurred.
2. Character belief: what a speaker thinks occurred.
3. Player knowledge: what the current playthrough has established.

Dialogue may express belief without converting it into player-confirmed fact. UI labels must distinguish testimony, evidence, inference, and confirmed fact.

## Compress responsibly

Prefer these translations in order:

1. Remove repetition while keeping the causal turn.
2. Combine locations while preserving event order and witnesses.
3. Turn inaccessible spectacle into sound, shadow, light, narration, or a symbolic prop.
4. Merge minor functions only when identity and knowledge remain coherent.
5. Add framing material only under an authorized adaptation mode.

Do not compress away the setup needed to understand a payoff.

## Shape playable rhythm

Alternate controlled performance with bounded agency. Use a typical loop:

```text
scene entrance → staged beat → interaction window → consequence beat → transition
```

Avoid stopping for input after every line. Open interaction when the player has a meaningful question, object, route, or relationship choice. Close the window only after a clear action and provide a visible way to continue.

## Direct actors through intention

Author high-level actions such as move to an anchor, face an actor, interrupt, withdraw, handle a prop, or hold silence. Let the runtime resolve coordinates and animation. Use gaze, distance, stillness, and prop handling to carry subtext instead of over-explaining it in dialogue.

## Control optional content

Optional conversations may deepen character, foreshadow, restate already known facts, or expose nonessential evidence. They must not be the sole route to a required fact unless the interaction is clearly required and reachable. They must obey character knowledge and reveal gates.

## End beats cleanly

Give every beat:

- An observable start trigger
- A dramatic purpose
- A bounded set of cues or actions
- Required state effects
- A completion or transition route
- A timeout/fallback for every blocking operation

Do not advance merely because a fixed duration elapsed if a required state change has not occurred.
