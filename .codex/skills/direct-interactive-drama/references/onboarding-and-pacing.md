# Onboarding and Pacing

## First-play contract

Within the opening sequence, establish through action:

1. Who the player is in relation to the immediate conflict.
2. Where they are and what feels wrong.
3. What they need now.
4. One core verb and its visible consequence.
5. The first dramatic question that motivates continued play.

Avoid a biography card or control dump. Pair each instruction with a situated action and remove it after success. Offer reminders through the journal, objective, or contextual prompt.

## Beat rhythm

Use the repeating form:

`orientation -> question -> action -> resistance -> consequence -> turn -> breath`

Intensity is a 0-5 authored target, not a claim about the player's emotion. Avoid more than two adjacent beats at the same intensity unless sustained stillness is the point. After a major reveal or high-intensity sequence, provide a low-demand beat for interpretation.

## Text discipline

- State only what the player cannot infer from visible action, sound, space, or behavior.
- Prefer short exchanges with changing tactics over uninterrupted exposition.
- Give optional dialogue a character or thematic purpose without making it the sole route to a required fact.
- Estimate reading time from text length and configured text speed; do not compress a scene to cue durations alone.
