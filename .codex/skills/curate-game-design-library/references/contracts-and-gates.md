# Contracts and gates

Use the formal schemas in `design-library/schemas/`; never invent record fields while researching. The required record flow is Scope → Work/Version → Source/Claim → Observation → Dossier → Pattern/Relation → Adapter/Index → Pack/Library release.

Gate ownership:

- G0: authority union, candidate reconciliation, scope decisions, version grouping.
- G1: included work and frozen-dimension coverage.
- G2: source tier, locator, claim type, critical A/B evidence, fact/interpretation closure.
- G3: pattern fields, five hooks, effect primitives, four department contracts, tests.
- G4: unique IDs, references, duplicate/relationship graph, observation disposition.
- G5-S at P4-P6: forbidden surfaces, transformation axes, structural deltas, clone-risk declarations, and production-index source isolation.
- G5-B at P8: independent blind attribution of the frozen complete game; pattern-level blind exercises before then are nonblocking diagnostics.
- G6–G9: selector relevance/abstention, 1+3 composition, canon, runtime and adapter fit.
- G13: exact release hashes and lock reproducibility.

Core dimension coverage is 100%; general dimension coverage is at least 95%. A `covered` cell needs an observation from the same work. A `not-applicable` cell needs a concrete dossier rationale and exactly zero observation references; do not attach a dimension weakly to avoid N/A.

Released cross-title patterns need at least two distinct works, multiple observations, counterevidence, `autoSelectable: true`, typed hooks, and fixture assertions. Single-title research remains non-selectable.
