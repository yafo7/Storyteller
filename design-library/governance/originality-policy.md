# Originality policy 1.0.0

The library transfers design principles, never Nintendo expression. Names, characters, lore, dialogue, maps, room layouts, puzzle step sequences, boss phase scripts, item silhouettes, icons, UI composition, musical motifs, sound cues, and recognizable visual staging are forbidden production inputs.

A released pattern must be supported by at least two works plus counterevidence and must survive transformation across at least three axes: worldbuilding, characters, objects, topology, feedback, narrative causality, timing, visual language, audio language, or control language. It also records structural deltas for topology graph, object-role graph, action sequence, feedback signature, and narrative function.

Flavor and clone risk are separate gates. High exploration or discovery flavor never offsets a recognizable source fingerprint. Raw source fingerprints stay in evidence/review context and are stripped from the production retrieval index. During P1-P6, forbidden-surface isolation, transformation axes, structural deltas, clone-risk declarations, and retrieval isolation are blocking. Pattern-level blind applications are retained as nonblocking historical diagnostics because they cannot validate a complete game's composition, art, staging, or player experience.

P8 owns the blocking whole-game blind attribution gate after P7 has produced a frozen playable candidate. Two independent reviewers are required; stable attribution of the shipped topology, object-role sequence, audiovisual staging, or encounter composition to one protected commercial source is a veto. The machine-readable phase boundary is authoritative in `originality-validation-policy.json`.

Single-title observations may remain research-only, but are never auto-selectable until cross-title support and counterevidence exist. The selector may abstain whenever meaningful transformation would destroy the story fit or leave insufficient originality margin.
