# Evidence policy 1.0.0

Keep source facts, analyst interpretations, comparisons, lineages, and analogies distinct. Every claim carries a type, criticality, version scope, precise locator, confidence, and review pair. A critical claim requires at least one tier A or B source. Community material may locate leads but cannot independently close a critical claim.

Source tiers are computed from source kind and publisher: A covers the game, official manuals and official pages; B covers attributable developer interviews, talks, and technical records; C covers academic or professional secondary analysis; D covers community leads. A source record never upgrades itself by analyst preference.

Use paraphrase and structured observation. Do not store walkthrough prose, maps, screenshots, music, dialogue, or long quotations. For a live or inaccessible primary source, `contentHash` fingerprints the retained citation snapshot/metadata record, not copyrighted binaries; the locator must still make the claim independently reproducible. Dynamic pages record access date and release-snapshot hash.

An observation describes context, precondition, player action, state transaction, perceptible feedback, and consequence. Its factual and interpretive halves cite different ledger claims. Conflicting evidence remains visible and contested; it is never averaged away.
